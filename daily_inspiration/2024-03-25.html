Environmental News of the Day 每日环境新闻

Datas shows that global ocean heat has hit a new record every single day for the last year, with the average ocean temperature 0.25 degrees Celsius warmer than previous year.

The temperature rise could be a result of the El-Nino effect as well as human-caused reasons.

El-Nino refers to the unusual warming of climate patterns in eastern tropical Pacific Ocean, which increase the surface water temperature.

A rise in sea temperature could result more frequent extreme weathers such as hurricanes and heat waves. Moreover, marine organisms such as coral reefs are made vulnerable due to such changes on their habitat.
