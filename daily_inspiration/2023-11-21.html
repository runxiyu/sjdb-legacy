The great civil war between Caesar and Pompey

For famine only makes a city free;
By gifts of food the tyrant buys a crowd
To cringe before him: but a people starved
Is fearless ever.

— Lucan

Quoted from The Pharsalia / De Bellum Civile, by Lucan. This book explores the themes of power, tyranny, and severed family bonds in the face of political avarice. Our familiar character, Julius Caesar, is portrayed in a way that contrasts to his usual glory and grandeur; instead, he is a bloodthirsty warmonger. There is no victor in a civil war.
