Environmental News of the Day 每日环境新闻

New satellite data shows that one-third of China's urban population is at risk of city-sinking.

Land subsidence, an overlooked urban hazard, is contributing to this phenomenon, along with rising sea levels.

Subsidence is mainly caused by human activities in cities, such as groundwater extraction and the weight of buildings.

Cities such as Tianjin and Shanghai are particularly affected, with Shanghai sinking by up to 3 meters over the past century.

Such research underscores the need for a national response.

Summarized from articles from Science Daily and UEA. Shared by Green Origin.

4月29日：世界舞蹈日、日本昭和之日

  • 1770 – On his first voyage, British explorer James Cook and the crew of HMS Endeavour landed at Botany Bay, making the first recorded European landfall on the eastern coast of Australia.

  • 1903 – A rockslide buried part of the Canadian mining town of Frank under 110 million tonnes of rock, killing around 70 people.
  • 1975 – Vietnam War: North Vietnam concluded its East Sea Campaign by capturing all of the Spratly Islands held by South Vietnam.
  • 2006 – Cyclone Mala made landfall near Thandwe, Myanmar, causing 37 deaths.
  • 1770年 – 英国探险家詹姆士·库克与努力号三桅帆船船员登陆悉尼海岸，为首批抵达澳大利亚东岸的欧洲人。
