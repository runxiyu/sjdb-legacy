November 1: Samhain and Beltane in the Northern and Southern Hemispheres, respectively; Rajyotsava (Formation Day) in Karnataka, India (1956)

  • 1503 – Giuliano della Rovere was elected pope, taking the name Julius II in emulation of Julius Caesar.
  • 1884 - The Gaelic Athletic Association was founded in County Tipperary.
  • 1921 - The first woman barrister in Ireland or Great Britain was called to the Bar of Ireland.
  • 1944 – World War II: An American F-13 Superfortress made the first flight by an Allied aircraft over Tokyo since the Doolittle Raid in April 1942.

Births and Deaths: Józef Zajączek (b. 1752); Paul Dickov (b. 1972); Anthony van Hoboken (d. 1983); Brian Lenihan Snr (d. 1995)
