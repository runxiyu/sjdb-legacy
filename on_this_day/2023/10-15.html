October 15

  • 1529 – Ottoman–Habsburg wars: The Siege of Vienna ended with Austrian forces repelling the invading Turks, turning the tide against almost a century of conquest in Europe by the Ottoman Empire.
  • 1917 – Dutch exotic dancer Mata Hari was executed by firing squad for spying for Germany.
  • 1965 – Vietnam War protests: At an anti-war rally in New York City, David J. Miller burned his draft card, the first such act to result in arrest under a new amendment to the Selective Service Act.
  • 1979 – A coup d'état in El Salvador overthrew and exiled President Carlos Humberto Romero before implementing a military junta.
  • 2018 – 13-year-old Jayme Closs was kidnapped from her home in Barron, Wisconsin, and held captive for 88 days.

Births and Deaths: Razia Sultana (d. 1240); Franklin Peale (b. 1795); Sai Baba of Shirdi (d. 1918); Kaikhosru Shapurji Sorabji (d. 1988)
